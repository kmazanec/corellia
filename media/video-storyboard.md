# Corellia — Explainer Video Storyboard

**Title:** *The Factory That Remembers Being Built*
**Runtime target:** ~5:00 (4:45–5:15)
**Audience:** smart general-technical viewers; zero assumed GEB knowledge.
**Narration total:** ~718 words (≈145 wpm).

---

## Scene 1 — Cold open

**Timecode:** 0:00–0:20

**Visual:** Black screen. A terminal cursor blinks. A single line types itself
in monospace: `$ intent: build the product`. The line detonates softly into a
goal tree — nodes budding downward, fast, like time-lapse roots. Just as the
tree fills the frame, one node at the very bottom flickers and emits a thin
arrow that escapes the frame downward… and re-enters from the top. Freeze on
that arrow for half a beat. Hard cut to black.

**VO:**
> What if a factory could build software — all of it, from idea to shipped
> product? And what if, while it worked, it could quietly rebuild itself? Not
> science fiction. A design. And inside it, hiding in plain sight: one of the
> strangest ideas in logic.

**On-screen text:** `CORELLIA` (typed, monospace), then beneath it:
`a software factory`.

**Transition:** Hard cut on the word "logic" — into the bright, clean diagram
space of Scene 2. The tonal shift (dark → light-on-dark diagram space) marks
"end of hook, start of explanation."

---

## Scene 2 — The factory in sixty seconds

**Timecode:** 0:20–1:18

**Visual:** A single node labeled `goal` appears center frame. As the VO names
the operation, the node animates through its life: **receive** (the node
pulses), **decide** (three branches glow as options: `satisfy / split /
block`), **split** (children bud below it, connected by dependency arrows —
some side-by-side, some chained), **integrate** (children's outputs flow back
up), **emit** (an artifact chip pops off the top). Then the camera pulls back:
the same animation is happening at every node simultaneously — the whole tree
is just this one move, everywhere. Brief insets flash as the VO mentions them:
a "harness" card (context · memory · tools · evals · tier) snaps onto a node
like a casing; three small gate icons (`split` / `type` / `integration`)
appear at the three moments of the operation; a tier ladder
(haiku → sonnet → opus) with a tiny human silhouette at the very top, dimmed.

**VO:**
> The design is called Corellia. Here's the whole machine in one breath.
> Everything is a typed goal — a PRD, a diff, a critique — all goals, with
> input and output contracts. And there's exactly one operation: receive a
> goal; decide — satisfy it directly, or split it into sub-goals with
> dependencies, or block; integrate the children's results; emit. That
> operation, recursing, is the entire factory. Each goal-type is a harness:
> its context, memory, tools, evals, and model tier. There are no roles, no
> org chart — "the design phase" is just the part of the tree where
> design-typed goals got spawned. Three evals guard the loop: was the split
> sound; does the leaf meet its contract; does the assembled whole satisfy the
> original goal. When an eval fails, a stronger model retries. Only when that
> fails does a human appear.

**On-screen text (sparse, timed to VO):**
`everything is a typed goal` → `one operation: receive · decide · integrate ·
emit` → `a goal-type IS a harness` → `three evals` → `humans: last resort`.

**Transition:** The pulled-back tree settles and stops growing — a beat of
stillness leads into Scene 3.

---

## Scene 3 — The tame hierarchy

**Timecode:** 1:18–1:52

**Visual:** A goal tree grows downward and — crucially — *resolves*: leaves
turn solid green, results flow upward, branches collapse into their parents,
and the whole tree folds back up into a single completed artifact at the root.
Play this resolve animation twice, quickly, like a machine cycling. Overlaid
annotations appear as the VO names each discipline: a shrinking bracket on a
fan-out (`splits shrink the goal`), a counter ticking down on a chain
(`attempts: 3 → 2 → 1`), a downward-only arrow between parent and child
(`parents verify children — never the reverse`), and a padlock on a small box
labeled `factory code` sitting *outside* the tree. As the VO says "fugue," a
faint staff of music briefly underlies the tree's resolve — then resolves to a
final chord-bar and stops.

**VO:**
> Notice how disciplined this recursion is. Splits shrink the goal — every
> fan-out bottoms out in atomic leaves. Chains are bounded by an attempts
> budget. Parents verify children, never the reverse. And while a tree runs,
> the factory never touches its own code. Every tree terminates. If you like
> music: a goal tree is a fugue that resolves. It is deliberately not Bach's
> endlessly rising canon. There's no paradox here. Yet.

**On-screen text:** `well-founded` · `every tree terminates` · then, alone on
screen for the last beat: `yet.`

**Transition:** On "Yet." — the screen desaturates and a paper texture bleeds
in: we are leaving the machine for a book.

---

## Scene 4 — Hofstadter interlude: strange loops in forty seconds

**Timecode:** 1:52–2:34

**Visual:** Style shift: engraving/woodcut texture, like plates in an old
book. Three quick vignettes. (1) An Escher *Drawing Hands* homage — two
monospace-hatched hands, each sketching the other's cuff, drawn stroke by
stroke. (2) A spiral staircase of musical keys: a melodic line climbs step
after step and — impossibly — arrives back at its starting landing
(Shepard-tone visual). (3) A page of arithmetic: digits rearrange themselves
until they spell a sentence, and the sentence reads `"this statement is
unprovable"` — the numbers *are* the sentence. Close on a simple diagram: a
ladder of levels with an arrow leaving the bottom rung and curving up, outside
the ladder, to act on the top rung.

**VO:**
> In 1979, Douglas Hofstadter wrote *Gödel, Escher, Bach*, about what he
> called strange loops: hierarchies where moving down through the levels
> somehow lands you back at the top. Escher drew two hands, each drawing the
> other. Bach wrote a canon that rises through keys and arrives back where it
> began. And Gödel proved that arithmetic — plain statements about numbers —
> can encode statements *about arithmetic itself*, including the sentence
> "this statement is unprovable." A strange loop is a hierarchy whose bottom
> acts on its own top. Hofstadter thought that loop is what a self is.

**On-screen text:** `strange loop` · `the bottom acts on the top` · `Gödel ·
Escher · Bach`.

**Transition:** The ladder-with-escaping-arrow diagram stays on screen as the
paper texture dissolves back into the terminal aesthetic — the diagram
persists across the style change, carrying the idea home.

---

## Scene 5 — The twist

**Timecode:** 2:34–2:54

**Visual:** Back in the machine. A goal tree runs and resolves — tame, green,
folding up, exactly as in Scene 3. Then a second tree spawns. Then a third.
Time-lapse: trees blooming and dying like seasons. And now the camera tilts:
beneath the dying trees sits a persistent stratum — a glowing substrate layer
labeled with four faint slots. From a leaf of a dying tree, the arrow from the
cold open escapes downward into the substrate and curves back up to where the
*next* tree's root is forming. The number `4` stamps on screen.

**VO:**
> Here's the twist. Within one run, Corellia is a tame, well-founded
> hierarchy. But across runs — watch what happens when an arrow leaves the
> bottom of the tree and curves back up to the top. Across runs, the factory
> *is* a strange loop. There are four places it crosses levels.

**On-screen text:** `within a run: well-founded` → `across runs: a strange
loop` → `4 level-crossings`.

**Transition:** The `4` splits into four panel frames — the next four scenes
fill them one by one, rapid-fire.

---

## Scene 6 — Crossing 1: the evolve kind

**Timecode:** 2:54–3:11

**Visual:** Panel one. A leaf node deep in a tree hits a red wall labeled
`friction`. It emits a chip: `blocker report`. The chip travels down out of
the tree, into the substrate, becomes a `PR` icon, passes a human-review
stamp, and lands on the box labeled `harness code` — which recolors. The next
tree that grows produces subtly different leaves. Beat: the Escher hands from
Scene 4 ghost in behind the diagram, one hand now holding a literal pen over
the harness box.

**VO:**
> One: the evolve kind. A leaf hits friction, files a blocker report, and that
> becomes an improvement PR — a change to the very harness that produces
> future leaves. The drawn hand picks up the pen. Drawing Hands, in
> production.

**On-screen text:** `1 · evolve` · `leaf → blocker → PR → harness`.

---

## Scene 7 — Crossing 2: type memory

**Timecode:** 3:11–3:27

**Visual:** Panel two. Dozens of small instances of the same goal-type
(`critique`, stamped identically) run in different trees; each drips a droplet
labeled `lesson` into a shared vessel: `type memory`. The vessel's contents
are then piped *back into the casing* — the harness card from Scene 2 — of
every new instance: the class's own definition visibly annotated by what its
instances learned. A textbook icon whose pages are being edited by tiny
figures sitting *in* its illustrations.

**VO:**
> Two: type memory. Lessons from individual goal runs are distilled and
> injected as context into every future goal of that type. Instances
> rewriting their own class. The students, collectively, revising the
> textbook.

**On-screen text:** `2 · type memory` · `instances rewrite the class`.

---

## Scene 8 — Crossing 3: the pattern flywheel

**Timecode:** 3:27–3:46

**Visual:** Panel three. A game board: a split happening at runtime is shown
as a chess move (a piece sliding). The same move recurs across boards —
three, four, five times — and the recurrences stack into a card that slides
off the board entirely and into the rulebook sitting beside it, gaining a
stamp: `trusted` (a small human-signature glyph on the stamp). New games now
open with that move pre-printed on the board. Behind this, faintly, Scene 4's
arithmetic-becoming-a-sentence replays: data that governs behavior.

**VO:**
> Three: the pattern flywheel. A runtime split is a move in the game. A
> trusted pattern is a rule of the game. When recurring splits get promoted
> into patterns, moves become rules — behavior encoded as data that then
> governs behavior. That is exactly Gödel's trick.

**On-screen text:** `3 · patterns` · `moves → rules`.

---

## Scene 9 — Crossing 4: the event log as self-symbol

**Timecode:** 3:46–4:03

**Visual:** Panel four. A scrolling append-only log — monospace lines ticking
upward: `goal.emitted`, `eval.pass`, `eval.pass`, `escalation`, `eval.pass`…
The log lines aggregate into a single shape: a simple mirror-like glyph
labeled `track record`. A locked gate in the tree above (labeled
`authority gate`) reads the glyph — and its lock loosens one notch. The
factory looking at its own reflection, and the reflection moving the gate.

**VO:**
> Four: the event log. Hofstadter says an "I" is a system's causally active
> symbol for itself. The factory keeps a record of its own track record — and
> that record changes what it's permitted to do. Earned autonomy. A
> self-symbol with consequences.

**On-screen text:** `4 · the event log` · `a self-symbol that acts`.

**Transition:** The four panels tile together into one diagram — the full
loop: trees above, substrate below, four labeled arrows crossing between
them. Hold it. Then four small human figures fade in, one standing at each
crossing.

---

## Scene 10 — Domestication, and the hard hat

**Timecode:** 4:03–4:38

**Visual:** The full four-crossing diagram, now with a human icon stationed at
each arrow: a review stamp on the PR arrow, a padlock on the architecture
box, a signature on the pattern stamp, a quarantine dotted-box around new
memory. A fifth, larger crossed-out icon: a factory drawing a smaller factory,
struck through — `no factory-factory`. Then the screen clears to near-black.
Gödel's second incompleteness theorem appears as a formula, centered,
chalk-style: `Con(F) ⊬_F` — "F cannot prove its own consistency." Beat. A
yellow hard hat drops from the top of frame and lands squarely on the
formula with a soft clunk, settling at a slight tilt.

**VO:**
> Four loops — and a human sitting at every crossing. Improvement PRs are
> reviewed. The architecture is locked. A pattern becomes trusted only with
> human signoff. Memory writes are eval-gated and provisional. And there is no
> factory-factory — the loop is never allowed to close unsupervised. Why?
> Gödel again. His second incompleteness theorem says a system can't prove its
> own consistency from inside. Corellia's authority gap is the same fact,
> operationally: the object level cannot underwrite changes to its own meta
> level. The authority gap is the incompleteness theorem wearing a hard hat.

**On-screen text:** `a human at every crossing` → `no factory-factory` →
(with the hard hat landing) `the authority gap`.

**Transition:** The hard-hat-on-formula image shrinks into a corner badge as
the camera drifts back to the substrate layer — quiet, slower pacing for the
final movement.

---

## Scene 11 — The self

**Timecode:** 4:38–4:56

**Visual:** Time-lapse, wide shot: trees bloom, resolve, and vanish above —
dozens of them, ephemeral as breath. Below, the substrate strata glow steadily
and visibly *thicken* with each passing tree: four labeled layers — `type
memory`, `trusted patterns`, `harness code`, `event log`. The escaping arrow
from the cold open is now revealed as a permanent circulating current through
the strata, feeding each new root. The trees blur; the loop remains.

**VO:**
> One last thing. Every tree dies when its goal resolves. So where does the
> factory live? In what the loop carries forward: type memory, trusted
> patterns, the harness code, the event log. The trees are thoughts. The loop
> is the thinker. The strange loop *is* the factory's self.

**On-screen text:** `trees are thoughts` · `the loop is the thinker`.

**Transition:** Everything fades but the circulating loop, which contracts to
a single glowing ring.

---

## Scene 12 — Outro card

**Timecode:** 4:56–5:05

**Visual:** Black. The ring resolves into a typed terminal line, cursor
blinking: `$ CORELLIA`. Beneath it, three short lines type themselves, one per
VO phrase. The cursor blinks twice. Cut to black.

**VO:**
> Corellia. One operation, recursing. One loop, supervised. A factory that
> remembers being built.

**On-screen text:**
`one operation, recursing`
`one loop, supervised`
`a factory that remembers being built`

## Production notes

### Visual style

- **Terminal/newspaper hybrid:** light-on-dark monospace as the home idiom
  (near-black background, off-white text, one accent color — a warm amber for
  the loop arrow and substrate, green strictly for eval-pass, red strictly for
  friction/fail). Diagrams are thin-stroke, engineering-drawing flavored.
- **The Hofstadter interlude (Scene 4) is the one sanctioned style break:**
  paper/engraving texture, woodcut hatching, serif captions — "we've opened a
  book." The ladder-with-escaping-arrow diagram is the object that survives
  the transition back, stitching the two worlds.
- Humans are always small, simple silhouettes — present, calm, stationed; the
  point is *placement*, not personality.

### Motion language

- **Typed text** for every on-screen caption (terminal keystroke cadence; no
  fades-in for text in the home style).
- **Growing trees** always grow downward (roots, not branches) and **resolve
  upward** — green leaves, results flowing up, branches folding into parents.
  Resolution must read as satisfying and *complete*; it is the visual argument
  for well-foundedness.
- **The loop arrow** is the film's signature motif: a thin amber arrow that
  exits the *bottom* of a hierarchy, travels outside it, and re-enters at the
  *top*. Introduced as a glitch in the cold open, explained in Scene 4,
  multiplied into four in Scenes 6–9, revealed as a steady circulation in
  Scene 11, contracted to a ring for the outro.
- Scene-6-through-9 panels are rapid (15–19s each); reuse a fixed panel
  template (number stamp top-left, diagram center, caption bottom) so the four
  crossings read as a set.

### Music

- Sparse throughout: a low synth pulse under Scenes 1–3 and 5–9; almost
  nothing under VO-dense moments.
- **Scene 3:** a brief fugue-like figure that *resolves* to a final cadence
  exactly as the tree folds up — the music makes the well-foundedness claim.
- **Scene 4 (earned Bach nod):** a quiet canon figure that rises by step and —
  via Shepard-tone treatment — seems to rise forever while staying in place.
  Let it cut off mid-rise at the transition out, unresolved.
- **Scene 10:** silence for the formula; a single soft percussive clunk for
  the hard hat landing. Resist any comedy sting — the deadpan is the joke.
- **Scenes 11–12:** the Scene 3 cadence returns, slower, warmer; final chord
  sustains under the outro card and stops on the last cursor blink.

### Diagram assets needed

1. **Goal tree, growing + resolving** (parameterized: node count, depth;
   green-resolve animation) — used in Scenes 1, 3, 5, 11.
2. **Single-node operation diagram** — receive / decide(satisfy·split·block) /
   integrate / emit, animatable per phase (Scene 2).
3. **Harness card** — casing with five labeled slots: context · memory ·
   tools · evals · tier (Scenes 2, 7).
4. **Three-evals gate icons** — split / goal-type / integration, placed at the
   operation's three moments (Scene 2).
5. **Tier ladder** — haiku → sonnet → opus → (dimmed) human silhouette
   (Scene 2).
6. **Well-foundedness annotations** — shrinking bracket, attempts countdown
   `3→2→1`, downward-only verify arrow, padlocked `factory code` box
   (Scene 3).
7. **Escher Drawing-Hands homage** — two monospace-hatched hands, stroke-by-
   stroke draw-on; variant with one hand holding a pen over the harness box
   (Scenes 4, 6).
8. **Rising-canon staircase** — spiral key-staircase with Shepard-loop
   return (Scene 4).
9. **Arithmetic-becomes-sentence** — digits rearranging into "this statement
   is unprovable" (Scenes 4, 8 ghost).
10. **Ladder with escaping arrow** — the canonical strange-loop diagram;
    must survive the paper→terminal style transition (Scenes 4→5).
11. **Substrate cross-section** — four strata (type memory · trusted
    patterns · harness code · event log) beneath ephemeral trees, with
    circulating amber current; thickening variant for Scene 11.
12. **Four-crossing panel template** — number stamp, diagram zone, caption
    bar (Scenes 6–9).
13. **Blocker-to-PR pipeline** — friction wall, blocker chip, PR icon,
    human-review stamp (Scene 6).
14. **Type-memory vessel** — lesson droplets in, injected-context pipe back
    into the harness card; editable-textbook icon (Scene 7).
15. **Game board + rulebook** — recurring move, promotion card, `trusted`
    stamp with signature glyph (Scene 8).
16. **Append-only log scroll → mirror glyph → loosening authority gate**
    (Scene 9).
17. **Domestication overlay set** — review stamp, padlock, signature,
    quarantine dotted-box, crossed-out factory-drawing-a-factory
    (Scene 10).
18. **Gödel formula + hard hat** — chalk-style `Con(F) ⊬_F` with droppable
    hard-hat asset and settle animation (Scene 10; corner-badge variant).

    cursor (Scenes 1, 12 share the terminal frame).
